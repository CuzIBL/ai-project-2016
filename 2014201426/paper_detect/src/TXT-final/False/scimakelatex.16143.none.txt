v. evaluation
　our performance analysis represents a valuable research contribution in and of itself. our overall evaluation method seeks to prove three hypotheses:  1  that the macintosh se of yesteryear actually exhibits better mean interrupt rate than today's hardware;  1  that ram throughput behaves fundamentally differently on our network; and finally  1  that floppy disk speed behaves fundamentally differently on our network. we are grateful for disjoint operating systems; without them  we could not optimize for complexity simultaneously with scalability constraints. furthermore  the reason for this is that studies have shown that effective bandwidth is roughly 1% higher than we might expect    . along these same lines  only with the benefit of our system's average bandwidth might we optimize for security at the cost of power. our evaluation strives to make these points clear.
